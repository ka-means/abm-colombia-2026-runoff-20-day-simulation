# Frame-balanced international and digital-amplifier methodology

## Correction introduced in v4

The previous international layer overrepresented rights-oriented and critical-hard-right
frames. The previous influencer layer overrepresented pro-Cepeda communities.

Version 4 balances the analytical sample by the observed frame of each individual piece.

## International frames

The model distinguishes:

- security, market and anti-Petro framing;
- economic-market framing;
- social-rights or progressive framing;
- institutional or descriptive framing;
- conflict-memory and human-rights framing;
- comparative polarisation framing.

An outlet is not assigned one permanent ideology. A Wall Street Journal news report,
a Wall Street Journal editorial and an Associated Press feature remain different
observations.

## Amplifier alignment

Each digital or editorial piece is classified as:

- favourable to De la Espriella or critical of Cepeda/Petro;
- favourable to Cepeda or critical of De la Espriella;
- mixed, interview-based or informational.

The classification applies to the piece, not to the permanent identity of the host,
programme or channel.

## Model use

Amplifier records modify reach, platform exposure, motif salience and social diffusion.
They do not directly change candidate preference.

Interviews are not endorsements unless explicit support is documented.

Missing likes, comments and views are unavailable values and must not be treated as zero.
