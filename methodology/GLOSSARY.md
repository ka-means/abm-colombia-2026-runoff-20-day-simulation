# Glossary

**ABM:** Agent-based model; a simulation in which individual entities follow rules and interact.

**Canonical signal:** The single master observation representing one underlying event cluster.

**Supporting observation:** Additional coverage of the same event, retained for provenance without counting it as an independent shock.

**Event cluster:** A group of observations referring to the same underlying event.

**Source-category balance weight:** Inverse-frequency experimental weight that equalises aggregate sampling mass across source categories.

**Model sampling weight:** Source-category weight divided by event-cluster size and capped at three.

**Information diet:** The subset and sequence of signals received by an agent.

**Motif memory:** Decaying memory of recurring narrative themes.

**Adaptive source trust:** Agent-specific trust that changes after exposure and interpretation.

**Information entropy:** A diversity/uncertainty measure applied to the daily information or decision distribution.

**Bounded confidence:** Interaction rule in which agents influence one another only within a defined distance or compatibility range.

**Cascade:** Multi-step transmission of a signal or narrative through network neighbours.

**Multiplex network:** Multiple network layers connecting the same population through different interaction contexts.

**Synthetic voting state:** Model category assigned to an agent; not an observed vote.

**Cognitive dossier:** Structured summary of an agent's attributes, exposures, memory, trust and final heuristic state supplied to the LLM.

**LLM deliberation:** Constrained model-generated interpretation of a cognitive dossier.

**Decision transition:** Difference between the final heuristic ABM state and the final LLM state.

**Language audit:** Automated checks over controlled synthetic sociolinguistic output.

**Source-strict:** A record whose interpretation is constrained to traceable source evidence and package contracts.

**Evidence support:** Internal score combining epistemic and credibility-related fields; not a legal or journalistic truth determination.

**Run profile:** Predefined simulation size and verbosity configuration: SMOKE, PILOT or FULL.
