# Assumptions and Limitations

## Principal modelling assumptions

1. A curated twenty-day information corpus can represent a controlled campaign information environment for mechanism testing.
2. Source-category inverse-frequency weights are useful experimental controls even though they do not estimate real exposure.
3. Event-cluster de-duplication is necessary to avoid counting repeated reporting as independent shocks.
4. Synthetic agents can be assigned heterogeneous access, trust, memory, uncertainty, mobilisation and network attributes for exploratory analysis.
5. Bounded direct and peer effects are preferable to deterministic signal-to-vote rules.
6. Four synthetic network layers can represent distinct interaction contexts at an abstract level.
7. Finite memory and adaptive source trust are relevant mechanisms in repeated information exposure.
8. Five final synthetic states are sufficient for this experimental comparison.
9. A dossier-constrained LLM decision can be analytically compared with a heuristic state without being interpreted as human cognition.
10. Controlled Colombian-Spanish markers can test variation and audit procedures, but not authentic sociolinguistic representation.

## Consequences of the assumptions

Changing any assumption can materially alter results. Therefore, model conclusions must be conditional: they describe what emerges under the documented rules, parameters, data package, seed and LLM protocol.

## Main limitations

- no probability sample of voters;
- no comprehensive census of campaign information;
- synthetic population and networks;
- modeller-defined behavioural equations;
- incomplete engagement metrics;
- potential annotation disagreement;
- source balance without guaranteed ideological or candidate balance;
- single-run headline outputs;
- remote LLM non-determinism and provider drift;
- stylised rather than authentic regional language;
- no claimed causal identification or predictive calibration.

Detailed ethical and political-use restrictions are in `ETHICS_AND_LIMITATIONS.md`. Required sensitivity tests are in `VALIDATION_PROTOCOL.md`.
