# Heuristic ABM Rules

## Purpose

This document describes the behavioural and scheduling contract of the heuristic agent-based layer. The executable notebook remains authoritative when implementation detail and prose diverge.

## Runtime profiles

| Profile | Agents | Use |
|---|---:|---|
| SMOKE | 30 | Structural and dependency testing |
| PILOT | 200 | Intermediate validation |
| FULL | 1,000 | Published experimental resolution |

The default seed is `42`.

## Temporal schedule

The simulation uses twenty daily steps from 1 June through 20 June 2026. For each day:

1. signals inside the active temporal window become eligible;
2. direct exposures are sampled for agents;
3. agents interpret signals through relevance and trust rules;
4. source trust and motif memory are updated;
5. selected narratives propagate through network layers;
6. social interaction modifies local states;
7. fatigue, mobilisation, uncertainty and propensities adapt;
8. a synthetic voting state is classified;
9. micro-events and macro-metrics are recorded.

## Principal configuration

The full-profile default includes:

- maximum two direct signals per agent per day;
- six-day active signal window;
- social-interaction sample of 0.25;
- direct-effect scale of 0.10;
- peer-effect scale of 0.030;
- memory decay of 0.88;
- adaptive source trust enabled;
- motif memory enabled;
- cascade maximum depth of three;
- cascade fan-out of two;
- cascade decay of 0.62;
- network rewiring disabled during the run.

The exact configuration is also stored in `configs/model_config.full.json`.

## Agent heterogeneity

Agents may differ in:

- macro-region, department and municipality;
- urban-system type;
- generation cohort;
- residential stratum and occupation/profile variables;
- ideological position and orientation dimensions;
- digital access and media-channel probabilities;
- source trust;
- susceptibility, confidence, uncertainty and fatigue;
- mobilisation, blank-vote and abstention propensities;
- semantic and motif memory;
- multiplex network position.

These are synthetic attributes and do not identify real individuals.

## Information environment

Each signal retains date, source category, event cluster, direction, candidate scope, motif, frame, emotion, intensity, credibility, misinformation risk, channels and relevant profiles.

Signals are not assigned directly to all agents. Eligibility and exposure depend on temporal availability, source visibility, channel access, relevance, sampling weight and stochastic selection.

## Relevance and interpretation

Interpretation is conditioned on the agent's existing position, profile, source trust, signal direction, candidate scope, motif, credibility, misinformation risk and accumulated state. Effects are bounded and scaled; one signal is not allowed to deterministically set the final state.

## Trust and memory

Source trust adapts after exposure rather than remaining completely fixed. Motif memory decays over time and accumulates repeated narrative exposure. Memory is finite and path-dependent.

## Multiplex networks

The model uses four social-network layers representing distinct interaction contexts. Layer-specific topology creates heterogeneous reach, local clustering and repeated exposure. Networks are synthetic and are validated structurally before the run.

## Cascades and peer effects

A selected signal may propagate through neighbours with bounded depth, fan-out and decay. Peer effects are weaker than direct effects by default. Transmission logs preserve source signal, sender, receiver, network layer, depth and effect information where available.

## Bounded-confidence interaction

Social interaction is conditional on agent similarity/distance rather than unlimited averaging. Interaction can reinforce, moderate or fail to move states depending on the agents' positions and confidence structure.

## End-of-day classification

The continuous internal state is translated into one of five synthetic categories:

- Cepeda;
- De la Espriella;
- Voto en blanco;
- No alineado;
- Abstencion.

Classification uses position together with uncertainty, mobilisation, blank-vote propensity and abstention propensity. The labels are model states, not observed ballots.

## Observation and emergence

Recorded outputs include state shares, entropy, polarisation, cascades, network agreement, source and motif effects, semantic dose, attribution, and transitions. Emergence is operationally defined as a macro-pattern generated by micro-level updates rather than imposed as a target.

## Reproducibility boundary

With the same input package, code, configuration and seed, the heuristic layer should reproduce exactly in a compatible environment. This expectation does not automatically extend to remote LLM inference.
