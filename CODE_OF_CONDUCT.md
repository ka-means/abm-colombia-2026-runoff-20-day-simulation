# Code of Conduct

Project discussions must remain professional, evidence-oriented, and respectful.

Contributors must:

- critique methods, code, assumptions, and evidence rather than individuals;
- avoid harassment, intimidation, discriminatory language, and personal attacks;
- distinguish documented facts from assumptions and interpretations;
- avoid using synthetic agent outputs to stereotype real demographic or regional groups;
- disclose conflicts of interest relevant to political, consulting, campaign, media, or funding relationships;
- protect credentials and any non-public information encountered during collaboration.

Maintainers may edit or remove contributions that violate these standards and may restrict participation when necessary to protect the project and its contributors.
